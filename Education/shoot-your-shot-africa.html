Shoot your shot Africa (Yabatech)...

Chisom Okpokwu & Vivian Nwanebo

Shoot Your Shot Afirca: KEMZ Mama, Edem Victor, Shola Animashaun, others educate Young Creatives

Shoot Your Shot Africa 3.0 held a one-day workshop to educate students on how to best use content creation tools such as photography, script writing, and mobile phones. The workshop, Emerging Creatives; A Festival Discovery for Building African Talent, was complemented with Workshops, Keynote speeches, Paniel Discussions, Talent Pitch, and Networking, and it was graced with dignitaries such as Kemz Mama (Actress), Edem Victor (Director), Shola Aminashun (Photographer), Anthony Kehinde (Musician), and others (Script Writer).

KEMZ MAMA

believes that young people should be self-sufficient, intellectual, and knowledgeable. She advised them to be consistent in their content creation and to learn how to create content in order to eliminate unemployment in society as a whole. "Don't give up; put your work out there and share your ideas." My first video was with Mr Macaroni, who saw me making videos with a friend in our estate using a borrowed phone because I had lost my phone and was using my mother's cloth. She went on.

"My first "Daddy wa"

content creation was with my mother's native material cloth." Your main priority should be to target the audience; if you can provide authentic content, do not hesitate, be hardworking, and committed. Concentrate on just one thing. She reached a conclusion. Scriptwriter Anthony Kehinde advised students on how to become a good scriptwriter, revealing some important characteristics a good writer should possess. "You must be a master of character, construction, and design." He stated.

Furthermore,

panelist Gbenga Salu stated, "Brace your African identity in a society where they are creating skills for the western world because you can't give the western world what they already have, do things that are truly your identity." He also expressed his views on the starting point in a society with many fillings from the Western world. It is all too easy for us to lose our African identity. As a director, Edem Victor advised the students "To be a good director, you must be willing to take risks. Students were counseled on their goals, which was anchored by Seun Adeleye, a film maker and producer. He advised students to discover your purpose. "The course of your research on people who know their purpose always sees opportunity. He stated.

"I am a professional problem solver because wherever I go,

if there is a problem, my heart tells me that there is money inside the problem. Some production companies discovered an elephant foot print in Epe a few weeks ago; when you find your purpose, you leave a footprint wherever you go, just like the elephant." Omoyibo Kelvin also gave the students advice on how to be better writers. "The best approach is to live the story you're telling, to aspire to be a genius, to learn from the greatest writers, to watch their interviews, to learn from their mistakes and successes, and to be internally optimistic. You must be willing to put yourself out there and accept criticism "He stated.

Adebisi Peter, CEO, Shoot Your Shot Africa,

described the accomplishment best: "It has been a long and difficult journey, but Shoot your shot Africa began because I wanted to provide a more level playing field for young creatives because it was difficult for me and other mentors. I wish we had smooth platforms for young creatives. I am a part-time lecturer at the institution of Yaba College Of Technology(Yabatech), and I hope the institution sees the value in what we are doing because my position has not improved so far." "The dream is bigger than this, but we want to take it little by little and manage the resources that we have, and we hope to be bigger and impact more lives as time goes on." He continued.
