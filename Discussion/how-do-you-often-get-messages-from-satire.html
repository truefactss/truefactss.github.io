Strike: A Foundament lookout for Students' Future

Uploaded 10/07/2022 by Abraham

Since the formation of the Academic Staff Union of Universities (ASUU) in 1978, the union has gone on strike sixteen times according to RNN.NG. Assault on academic freedom was a subject of resistance by ASUU when the body started operation, the establishment of the body was primarily concerned with broad national issues such as illegal dismissal of staffs whether they are lecturers or not, a good example was when they supported NMA (National Medical Association) and NARD (National Association of Resident Doctors) to protest the sharp deterioration of Nigeria's health services, the body also stood firmly against oppressive, undemocratic policies in the country. Throughout the military period, ASUU fought for: i. the survival of the university system, with three components: service conditions (salary and non-salary), funding, and university autonomy/academic freedom; the defense of the right to education; and ii. broad national issues such as anti-military struggles, struggles against military rule, struggles against privatization, struggles against the Structural Adjustment Programme (SAP), and World Bank attestation. Although it looked after the welfare of its members in the social, political, and economic worlds, it also contributed to the stagnation of education in Nigeria. Nigeria's educational sector is very cheap, making it easier for the country to have a higher number of literates than other African countries; however, most of the promises made by the government to the body since 2009 have not been met, forcing the body to protest by striking. In the last six years, there has been one strike every four days, with the longest strike occurring in 2020 during the covid-19 pandemic.

This period was supposed to be productive

and help lecturers teach students some theoretical aspects as it would aid time management and easy access to all students, but most students were at home doing nothing, according to research. The Association of Staff Unions met with the federal government in 2009 to demand better working conditions, but their request fell on deaf ears. The body's national executive council has also stated that there will be no suspension of the ongoing strike until the federal government's renegotiated agreement is fully implemented. During the two-month strike, Mr Chukwuemeka Nwajiuba, the current Minister of Education (state), purchased a presidential nomination form worth 100 million naira. Most students are now disappointed and angry because they are spending more years than they should in school. In an interview with an angry University of Lagos student, she stated, "I'm honestly disappointed with the leaders in this country, looking at the aspect of funding and accepting the conditions of ASUU, we would understand that this country as a whole is a mess, there's no money to give ASUU, but they spent billions of naira on the NUGA games in unilag, this is bad." "I hope this strike ends soon, it's affecting our future," said Miss Victoria Ojo of the same school.
