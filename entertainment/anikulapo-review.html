Ànikulapo

Ayomide Kolawole

SÀRÓ ALASO ÒFÌ LATI GBÒGÁN.

In Oyo empire where the movie was set was seen to be headed by a constitutional monarch. In Oyo, there is a warm welcoming. Where strangers are hospitalized, they are well known by the generosity but something was missing. If you give a thief shelter and put food on his table, he would still sip your water without your consent. Following the story of a picaro persona, sàró aso-òfì from the village of Gbògán. Immediately sàró stepped his foot into the Oyo empire, he should have encounter an unforgettable memory which would keep appearing in his head rather than how he get reminded of how he grew up and how he But rather, he was glady welcome by Àwárùn, an Oyomesi who was into pottery business. Sàró totally forgot his hard days and craves for better days when he saw the cool and calm place to lay his head. When Sàró was warned by a young man, he didn't listen. He turned a deaf ear to his advice. Sàró, who supposed to have listened refused.

"When you choose an action, be ready to live with it consequences"

Àwárùn, who's Sàró's refuge made the very first mistake by letting sàró enjoyed the sound of the night, she shouldn't have lured him to the hut of enjoyment where he enjoys the moans and sexual pleasures. Why shouldn't he face the toughest times of his life before getting the sweetest part of it? And when he finally met the opportunity to be the person he craves for he? He ruined it!

“Itelorun baba ìwa"

Fate had had it written when he met a soul whose her story is also similar to his, a woman that's priceless. Why shouldn't he have the contentment that would have driven his everlasting Joy to his life and leaves the audience not in despair? When Àwárùn warned him, he shouldn't have happened like he adhered, which means his fortune should have locate him when he was still in Oyo empire. Before he could elope with Aròlàkè, he was beaten and given the chance to see the other side of life. As it should be, Aròlàkè helped him. She took the Àdó which dropped from the mouth of Àkàlà bird. A bird which has the power of resurrection. When sàró regained his conscious, why shouldn't he be contented with Aròlàkè alone and make Aròlàkè enjoy the pleasure of motherhood? A teenage girl who has been inherited by the King, when she finally found the person who lingers her desire, he failed her. Aròlàkè should have been given a child and let her see someone smile with and sàró should have given an opportunity to be an angel to people with help of the Àdó.

“itelorun baba ìwa"

sàró was not given the chance to be called the divine name 'Ànikulapo' and he renegade the words of the elders which says, “ikún n je ògèdè, ikún n rèdí. Ikún o mò pe òun to dùn ni pani" Sàró should have been given a chance to be a satisfied soul and be called 'Ànikulapo' Aròlàkè should have been given a child, “kí èmi nan rí omo ke."
